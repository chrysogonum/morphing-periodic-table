What Newlands saw — and where it broke

In 1865 John Newlands lined the known elements up by atomic weight and noticed that properties seemed to repeat every eighth one — a "law of octaves," like a musical scale. The Royal Society refused to publish it and a colleague asked whether he'd tried arranging them alphabetically. Here is his claim rebuilt on this instrument: the 63 elements known in 1865, ordered by weight, with atomic volume as height (Lothar Meyer's 1870 curve) and chemical family as color. Watch the octave hold — then watch exactly where it fails.
